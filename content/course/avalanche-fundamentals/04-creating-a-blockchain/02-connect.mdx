In the final section of this chapter, we will go over the process of connecting your wallet to your Subnet along with sending some funds.

## Connecting to Your Subnet

### Using AvaCloud

Make sure your wallet is connected to AvaCloud. Then, recall the following page from last time:

![](/course/avalanche-fundamentals/24.png)

We can simply navigate to the Subnet's details we require for connection, by clicking the **arrow** and then the **Details** tab. There click the **Add to Wallet** button.

![](/course/avalanche-fundamentals/25.png)

### Manual Connection

If for some reason the button should not work with your wallet, you can also add you Subnet manually. In particular, there are two fields that we care about: the Chain ID and the RPC Url; these fields are what will allow us to connect our wallets to AvaCloud. To start, open Core Wallet, head into the **Networks** tab, and click the + button, where you should see the following screen:

![](/course/avalanche-fundamentals/26.png)

Congrats, you've just connected to your own Subnet!

## Interacting with Your Subnet

You should now be able to see the airdrop for your admin address in the you wallet when you open it:

![](/course/avalanche-fundamentals/27.png)

Click the Actions button and transfer some of the tokens to a random address (e.g. 0x321f6B73b6dFdE5C73731C39Fd9C89c7788D5EBc) to verify that this is actually a fully operational blockchain.
